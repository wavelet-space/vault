//Hello. I want my own local copy of your Widget that I will manipulate, //but I don’t want my changes to affect the one you have. I may or may not //hold onto it for later, but that’s none of your business. void foo(Widget w);

//Hello. I want to take your Widget and play with it. It may be in a //different state than when you gave it to me, but it’ll still be yours //when I’m finished. Trust me! void foo(Widget& w);

//Hello. Can I see that Widget of yours? I don’t want to mess with it; //I just want to check something out on it. Read that one value from it, //or observe what state it’s in. I won’t touch it and I won’t keep it. void foo(const Widget& w);

//Hello. Ooh, I like that Widget you have. You’re not going to use it //anymore, are you? Please just give it to me. Thank you! It’s my //responsibility now, so don’t worry about it anymore, m’kay? void foo(Widget&& w);
